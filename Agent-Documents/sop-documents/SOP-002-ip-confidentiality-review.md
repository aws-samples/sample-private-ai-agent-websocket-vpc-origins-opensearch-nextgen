# SOP-002: Intellectual Property and Confidentiality Review

**Document ID:** SOP-IP-002  
**Version:** 1.8  
**Last Updated:** April 2026  
**Owner:** Example Corp — Legal Department  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

---

## 1. PURPOSE

This SOP defines Example Corp's mandatory requirements for intellectual property ownership, licensing, and confidentiality protections in all partner contracts. Deviations from these standards constitute review findings.

---

## 2. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY OWNERSHIP

### 2.1 Work Product Ownership (Non-Negotiable)
- All deliverables created specifically for Example Corp must be owned by **Example Corp**
- "Work made for hire" language must be present for custom deliverables
- Assignment clauses must cover all work product, including derivative works

### 2.2 Pre-Existing IP
- Provider may retain ownership of pre-existing tools and methodologies
- Pre-existing IP must be explicitly listed in a schedule/exhibit
- Example Corp must receive a **perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free license** to any pre-existing IP incorporated into deliverables

### 2.3 Prohibited IP Terms — Flag as CRITICAL
- Provider retains ownership of custom deliverables created for Example Corp
- Revocable licenses to deliverables
- Licenses conditioned on ongoing payments after contract completion
- Restrictions on Example Corp modifying or building upon delivered work
- Provider rights to reuse Example Corp-specific work product for other clients

---

## 3. LICENSE REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Minimum License Terms for Example Corp
If any component is licensed rather than assigned:
- **Perpetual** (survives termination)
- **Irrevocable** (cannot be withdrawn)
- **Worldwide** (no geographic restriction)
- **Royalty-free** after contract payments complete
- **Sublicensable** to Example Corp subsidiaries and affiliates
- Right to modify and create derivative works

### 3.2 License Red Flags
Flag the following as HIGH severity:
- "Non-transferable" licenses for core deliverables
- Time-limited licenses
- Revocable at provider's discretion
- "Internal use only" restrictions on paid deliverables
- Licenses tied to ongoing subscription/maintenance fees

---

## 4. CONFIDENTIALITY REQUIREMENTS

### 4.1 Survival Period (Mandatory Minimums)
- **Trade secrets:** Indefinite / as long as information qualifies
- **Business/financial information:** Minimum **5 years** post-termination
- **Technical information:** Minimum **3 years** post-termination
- General confidentiality clause: Minimum **3 years**

### 4.2 Confidentiality Protections Required
All contracts must include:
- Clear definition of what constitutes Confidential Information
- Restriction to "need to know" basis
- Written consent required before sharing with any third party including subcontractors
- Return or destruction of confidential materials upon termination
- Notification obligation if disclosure is legally compelled

### 4.3 Prohibited Confidentiality Terms — Flag as HIGH
- Survival period less than 3 years
- Permission to share with subcontractors without written consent
- No return/destruction obligation
- Overly broad exclusions that gut the protections
- Unilateral definition of what is "not confidential"

---

## 5. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICIT

### 5.1 Acceptable Terms
- Non-solicitation of employees: Maximum 12 months post-termination
- Non-compete (if any): Must be narrowly scoped to specific project domain
- Geographic restrictions must be reasonable

### 5.2 Flag for Review
- Non-compete exceeding 12 months
- Overly broad non-compete covering all Example Corp business areas
- Non-solicit without reciprocal obligations

---

## 6. SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION

- **CRITICAL:** Provider owns deliverables Example Corp paid for; revocable licenses
- **HIGH:** Confidentiality survival under 3 years; third-party sharing without consent
- **MEDIUM:** Missing non-solicit clause; license scope ambiguities
- **LOW:** Formatting issues in IP schedules

---

## 7. AGENT REVIEW INSTRUCTIONS

When reviewing IP and confidentiality terms:
1. Identify who owns custom deliverables — must be Example Corp
2. Check license type (perpetual, irrevocable, etc.)
3. Verify confidentiality survival period meets minimums
4. Confirm third-party sharing requires written consent
5. Check for return/destruction obligations
6. Flag any revocable or time-limited licenses as CRITICAL
7. Generate findings with clause reference and corrective language
